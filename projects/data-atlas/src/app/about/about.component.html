TerraNova digital atlas of Europe is being compiled by an interdisciplinary group of researchers that combine human population patterns in the past, plants and disturbances, animal development, and climate change. This atlas will give strategic guidelines and policy measures for politicians and landscape practitioners, prove the strength of interdisciplinary research in academia in the Anthropocene and to raise sustainable awareness on landscape reform among academia, the commerce and the general public.
